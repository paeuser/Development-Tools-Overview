AI Fundamentals

Welcome to the AI Fundamentals page. Here, you will find resources and information to help you understand the basics of Artificial Intelligence, including:

  • Machine Learning: A subset of AI that enables systems to learn and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed.
    • Netflix: Recommends shows based on what you’ve watched.
    • Spam Filters: Detect junk email.
    • Self-Driving Cars: Recognize stop signs, people, and other cars.
    • Banking: Detect fraud by spotting unusual activity.
  • Deep Learning: A specialized branch of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers to analyze complex data.
  • Neural Networks: Computational models inspired by the human brain, consisting of layers of interconnected nodes.
  • Supervised Learning: A type of machine learning where the model is trained on labeled data.
  • Unsupervised Learning: A type of machine learning that identifies patterns in data without labeled examples.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): Techniques for enabling machines to understand and process human language.
  • Computer Vision: The field of AI focused on enabling machines to interpret and analyze visual data.
  • Reinforcement Learning: A type of machine learning where agents learn by interacting with their environment to maximize rewards.
  • Ethics in AI: Discussion on the ethical implications and responsible use of AI technologies.
  • ML Model (Machine Learning Model): A program or algorithm that learns patterns from data to make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed for every scenario.
  • DS Model (Data Science Model): A data-driven analytical model—often using statistical or machine learning techniques—to extract insights, identify trends, or solve business problems.

Microsoft Customer Facing Software Engineer (CFSE) Role

Overview

The Microsoft Customer Facing Software Engineer (CFSE) is a hybrid role that combines software engineering with direct customer engagement. As a CFSE, you work closely with enterprise clients to build, deploy, and optimize technical solutions using Microsoft’s platforms and tools.

Key Responsibilities

  • Collaborate with customers to understand their technical challenges and design tailored solutions.
  • Develop and deliver software solutions, integrations, and proof-of-concept projects.
  • Advise on architecture, scalability, and best practices across Microsoft technologies.
  • Provide hands-on coding support and technical mentorship to customer teams.
  • Work with internal Microsoft engineering teams to share feedback and improve products.

Why This Role Stands Out

  • Direct interaction with enterprise-level customers and decision-makers.
  • A blend of consulting, software development, and cloud architecture.
  • Impactful work that shapes how customers use Microsoft technology.
  • Typically allows remote or hybrid flexibility, depending on the team.

Common Skills and Tools

  • Strong software engineering background (C#, Python, JavaScript, etc.).
  • Deep knowledge of Azure services (e.g., App Services, Functions, AKS, DevOps).
  • Cloud architecture and deployment experience.
  • Strong communication and collaboration skills.
  • Understanding of DevOps, CI/CD, and security best practices.

Typical Interview Focus Areas

  • Technical deep dives: coding, design, and debugging challenges.
  • Behavioral questions around customer interactions and teamwork.
  • Scenarios focused on architectural decision-making and trade-offs.
  • Knowledge of Microsoft tools and ecosystems (especially Azure).

Personal Experience: Insights from a CFSE

I had a friend who worked as a Customer Facing Software Engineer (CFSE) at Microsoft. He said it was a great role that allowed him to work closely with customers while also doing hands-on coding and development work. He really enjoyed the mix of technical challenges and customer interaction.

Before, the role was called Premier Field Engineer (PFE) and was more focused on support. Now, it has evolved to include more proactive solution development and customer engagement. This change was made to align with the market and better support sales teams.

He used to help customers adopt Microsoft products, working primarily with Microsoft 365, especially SharePoint and Power Apps. He was very passionate about his job—something typical of Microsoft employees.

When I asked him what it was like to work as a CFSE, he emphasized the opportunity to work closely with customers and the hands-on coding and development work. He enjoyed the blend of technical challenges and customer interaction.

Typical Routine

  • Helping customers overcome roadblocks when using Microsoft products
  • Delivering training, such as Power Apps workshops
  • Advising on governance implementation for Teams
  • Occasionally taking phone calls to answer questions or provide guidance
  • Collaborating with customers to plan how they want to use Microsoft technologies

There are different groups within Microsoft for which these positions exist, such as Teams, SharePoint, and Power Apps.

Key Terms Explained

  • Waterfall: A traditional project management method where each phase (like planning, design, development, testing) happens one after the other, with little overlap. You finish one step before moving to the next.
  • Agile: A modern, flexible way to manage projects. Work is done in small, repeatable cycles (called sprints), allowing teams to adjust quickly and deliver updates often.
  • Bootstrap: A popular toolkit for building websites quickly. It provides ready-made styles and components (like buttons, forms, and layouts) so web pages look good on all devices.
  • WCMS platforms (Web Content Management Systems): Software that helps people create, edit, and manage website content easily, without needing to code. Examples include WordPress, Drupal, and Joomla.

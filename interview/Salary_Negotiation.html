Salary Negotiation Guide

1) Salary Expectation Question: When a recruiter calls, it is NOT the time to negotiate. DON'T NEGOTIATE.

  • Do not bring up your past salary. The recruiter wants to close you as soon as possible.
  • What you say now might be too high or too low; you can always discuss numbers later in the process once you have more information from the hiring manager.
  • It's important to discover the salary range first.
  • If asked about your expectations, respond that it's too early in the process and there are many variables at play. After learning more about the company, the role, and the scope of the job, you’re confident you can reach an agreement together.
  • Sample response: "It's a bit early in the process—there are a lot of variables at play. Once I learn more about the company, the role, and the scope, I'm sure we can agree on something that's fair for both sides."
  • Then, start asking questions:
    • What is your budget and salary band for this role?
    • In some states like California, employers are required to provide this range/band.
  • What is a salary band? A salary band is a defined pay range for a job role that sets the minimum, midpoint, and maximum salary based on factors like experience, performance, and market rates.

2) Find Out Their Leverage: Just as you have leverage, learn about theirs:

  • How long has this position been open?
  • What is the budget for this role?
  • Are they raising a new round of equity?
  • Is there a hiring freeze?
  • How is the offer decision approved?
  • What is the company's compensation philosophy?
  • What are some comparable levels at companies like Meta or Google? (For example, they might say this is an E5 at Meta.)

Explanation of Engineering Levels & Equity

Meta (formerly Facebook) Engineering Levels:

  • E3 – Entry-Level Software Engineer (new grad or 0–1 years of experience)
  • E4 – Software Engineer (1–3 years of experience)
  • E5 – Senior Software Engineer (typically 3–6 years)
  • E6 – Staff Software Engineer (more experienced senior engineer)
  • E7 – Senior Staff Engineer
  • E8+ – Engineering Director / Principal Engineer / Leadership roles

Why does level matter? Figuring out what level you are interviewing for is important, as it directly impacts your compensation and responsibilities.

  • E3 – Approx. $192K
  • E4 – Approx. $250K
  • E6 – Approx. $650K
  • E9 – Approx. $2.53M

3) Process: Ask about the timeline between the first interview and receiving an offer, and when you’ll get specific details on equity and salary. This helps you identify key decision-makers and plan your negotiation strategy.

Salary negotiation is a key skill for maximizing your compensation and career satisfaction. Here are practical steps and tips to help you negotiate your next offer with confidence.

Before the Offer

  • Research salary ranges for your role, location, and experience (use sites like Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, or LinkedIn Salary).
  • Know your minimum acceptable salary and your ideal target.
  • Prepare a list of your accomplishments and unique skills.
  • Practice your pitch and responses to common negotiation questions.

During Negotiation

  • Let the employer make the first offer if possible.
  • Express enthusiasm for the role and company.
  • Ask for time to review the offer before responding.
  • Politely and confidently counter with your target salary (use data to justify your ask).
  • Consider negotiating for other benefits: bonus, PTO, remote work, signing bonus, education, etc.
  • Get all offers and changes in writing.

Sample Negotiation Script

"Thank you for the offer! Based on my research and experience, I was expecting something in the range of $X–$Y. Is there flexibility to increase the base salary or add a signing bonus?"

Be polite, direct, and always express appreciation for the opportunity.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Accepting the first offer without negotiation.
  • Revealing your minimum or current salary too early.
  • Negotiating aggressively or emotionally.
  • Not considering the full compensation package.
